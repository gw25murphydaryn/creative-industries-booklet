Unit 1 – Creative Industries: An Introduction Tasks 1–3
Task 1 – Creative Industries Sectors

Task Description – What, Why, How

What: You will explore different sectors within the Creative Industries and identify what they produce, who works in them, and how they contribute to the wider creative economy.

Why: Understanding the sectors helps you see the scale of the industry, the variety of roles available, and how creative work connects to real‑world audiences and businesses.

How: Choose two sectors. For each one, describe what they make, who works there, and why the sector matters. Use examples from real companies or job roles to support your answers.

Learning Intention

I am learning to identify and describe key sectors within the Creative Industries.

Success Criteria

  • I can name different Creative Industries sectors.
  • I can describe the products and services they provide.
  • I can give examples of jobs or companies in each sector.
  • I can explain why each sector is important.
Task 2 – Legal & Safety Report

Task Description – What, Why, How

What: You will research the legal, ethical, and safety requirements that affect creative work in your chosen sector.

Why: Laws and safety guidelines protect creative workers, clients, and audiences. Knowing them helps you work professionally and responsibly.

How: Choose one Creative Industries sector. Research relevant laws, safety rules, and supporting organisations. Explain what they are and why they matter for people working in that sector.

Learning Intention

I am learning to understand the laws and safety guidelines that protect creative workers and audiences.

Success Criteria

  • I can identify relevant laws and regulations.
  • I can explain why these laws matter.
  • I can describe organisations that support creative workers.
  • I can explain how legal and safety issues affect creative work.
Task 3 – Employment Opportunities

Task Description – What, Why, How

What: You will investigate different types of employment in the Creative Industries and compare the benefits and challenges of each.

Why: Understanding employment types helps you plan your future and recognise how creative workers build careers.

How: Choose at least two job roles. For each one, describe the role, the type of employment, and any challenges (especially for freelancers). Use real examples where possible.

Learning Intention

I am learning to understand different employment types and what they mean for creative workers.

Success Criteria

  • I can describe different job roles.
  • I can identify employment types (e.g., freelance, full‑time).
  • I can explain the challenges freelancers may face.
  • I can describe the responsibilities of each role.
Unit 2 – Skills Development Tasks 4–6
Task 4 – Practical Skills Audit

Task Description – What, Why, How

What: You will assess your current creative skills and identify your strengths and areas for improvement.

Why: Being honest about your skills helps you plan your next steps and prepare for real creative work.

How: Choose a creative job role. List key skills needed for that role, rate your current level, and explain why you gave yourself that rating.

Learning Intention

I am learning to evaluate my own creative skills honestly and accurately.

Success Criteria

  • I can identify skills needed for a chosen job role.
  • I can rate my current level of experience.
  • I can explain why I rated myself this way.
  • I can identify areas I want to improve.
Task 5 – Target Setting

Task Description – What, Why, How

What: You will set realistic, achievable targets to help you develop your creative skills.

Why: Clear targets give you direction and help you measure your progress over time.

How: Based on your skills audit, write at least three specific targets. For each one, explain why it matters and how you will know you have achieved it.

Learning Intention

I am learning to set clear and achievable targets for my own development.

Success Criteria

  • I can write clear, specific targets.
  • I can explain why each target is important.
  • I can link my targets to my chosen job role.
  • I can identify how I will measure my progress.
Task 6 – Approaches to Skill Development

Task Description – What, Why, How

What: You will plan practical ways to develop your creative skills, including where, when, and how you will practise.

Why: Planning realistic activities helps you turn your targets into real progress.

How: Choose at least two methods for developing your skills (e.g., practice tasks, online tutorials, workshops). For each method, describe what you will do, where, when, and what support or equipment you need.

Learning Intention

I am learning to plan realistic activities that will help me improve my creative skills.

Success Criteria

  • I can describe methods for developing my skills.
  • I can identify where and when I will practise.
  • I can list the equipment and support I need.
  • I can explain why these methods will help me improve.
Unit 3 – The Creative Process Tasks 7–9
Task 7 – Personal Response to a Brief

Task Description – What, Why, How

What: You will respond to a creative brief by developing your own idea and explaining how it meets the needs of the client and audience.

Why: Being able to respond to a brief is a key skill in the Creative Industries. It shows you can work to requirements while still being creative.

How: Read the brief carefully. Describe your idea, who it is for, and how it meets the brief. Consider costs, timescales, safety issues, and the skills and resources you will need.

Learning Intention

I am learning to develop and justify a creative idea based on a brief.

Success Criteria

  • I can describe my idea clearly.
  • I can identify the target audience.
  • I can explain how my idea meets the brief.
  • I can consider costs, timescales, and safety issues.
  • I can identify the skills and resources needed.
Task 8 – Feedback Reflection

Task Description – What, Why, How

What: You will reflect on feedback you have received and explain how it helped you improve your work.

Why: Feedback is an important part of the creative process. Learning to use it well helps you develop stronger ideas and outcomes.

How: Choose a piece of feedback you received on your work. Describe what the feedback was, how you responded, and what changes you made or could make as a result.

Learning Intention

I am learning to use feedback to improve my creative ideas and outcomes.

Success Criteria

  • I can describe the feedback I received.
  • I can explain how I responded to it.
  • I can identify improvements I made or could make.
  • I can reflect on how feedback supports creative development.
Task 9 – Team Roles & Tasks

Task Description – What, Why, How

What: You will explore how creative teams work together and plan how tasks can be shared fairly.

Why: Most creative projects involve teams. Understanding roles and responsibilities helps you work effectively with others.

How: List your team members and their roles. Describe the key tasks in your project and explain who will do what, and why this is a fair and effective way to share the work.

Learning Intention

I am learning to understand team roles and how responsibilities can be shared.

Success Criteria

  • I can identify team members and their roles.
  • I can describe key tasks in a project.
  • I can explain how tasks can be shared fairly.
  • I can describe how teamwork supports creative projects.
Unit 4 – Creative Project Tasks 10–12
Task 10 – Project Planning Sheet

Task Description – What, Why, How

What: You will plan the key tasks, resources, and timescales needed to complete your creative project.

Why: Good planning helps you stay organised, meet deadlines, and produce a stronger final outcome.

How: Give your project a clear title. List the main tasks, the resources you need, your role, and realistic timescales and review dates.

Learning Intention

I am learning to plan a creative project effectively.

Success Criteria

  • I can identify key tasks.
  • I can list the resources I need.
  • I can describe my role in the project.
  • I can plan realistic timescales and review points.
Task 11 – Mid‑Project Review

Task Description – What, Why, How

What: You will review your progress so far and identify what is going well and what needs to change.

Why: Reviewing your progress helps you spot problems early and make realistic adjustments.

How: Reflect on your project so far. Describe what is going well, what problems or delays you have faced, and what changes you will make next.

Learning Intention

I am learning to evaluate my progress and make realistic adjustments.

Success Criteria

  • I can describe what is going well.
  • I can identify problems or delays.
  • I can explain what changes I will make.
  • I can reflect on how these changes will help.
Task 12 – Final Evaluation

Task Description – What, Why, How

What: You will evaluate your final project outcome and reflect on your skills and performance.

Why: Evaluating your work helps you recognise your achievements and identify what to improve next time.

How: Reflect on your final product and your role in the project. Describe what went well, what could be improved, how you worked in the team or on your own, and what skills you developed.

Learning Intention

I am learning to evaluate my creative work and reflect on my development.

Success Criteria

  • I can describe what went well.
  • I can identify what could be improved.
  • I can reflect on how well I worked independently or in a team.
  • I can identify the skills I developed.
